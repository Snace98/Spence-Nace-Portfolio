About Me

Previous Next

When I am not coding, I like to spend my time playing and listening to music, exploring outdoors, and spending time with my friends. As a self-taught guitarist, I am passionate about learning as much as I can to become more expressive with my playing. This ongoing experience has helped me develop my self-learning skills by instilling in me the value of practice; demonstrating the fruitful results of thoughtful experimentation; and helping me learn to combine my own ideas with those of others to create something unique. These same lessons apply to my software development journey as my enthusiasm to create interactive applications and quality code drives me to pick up new skills, languages, and coding practices.

During college, I was awarded the opportunity to study abroad in Greece through an essay writing scholarship. This experience allowed me to expand my understanding of different cultures throughout history and allowed me to witness inspiring human-made and natural beauty. My combined interest in the humanities and coding uniquely positions me to think critically and creatively about problems, utilizing a variety of intellectual and skill based resources.
